## 2. Interest Rate Risk (The Big Problem)

### Why Mortgage REITs Are Risky

AGNC invests in **agency mortgage-backed securities (MBS)**. Here's the problem:

| Rate Scenario | What Happens to AGNC |
|---------------|---------------------|
| Rates FALL | MBS prices rise, but AGNC's portfolio yields become "locked in" lower than new originations. Book value can drop. |
| Rates RISE | MBS prices fall, but AGNC can buy higher-yielding mortgages. Net interest income improves. |
| Yield Curve Flattens | Hurts spread income. |

### Current Environment (April 2026)
- Fed has been cutting rates
- 10-year Treasury yields have been volatile
- **If rates continue falling → AGNC price could decline**

**This is the core risk:** You might hold AGNC for a week, and a Fed announcement or Treasury move could tank it 5-8% in a day.

## 4. The Honest Verdict

### Can you hold AGNC for a week? 
**Technically yes** — it's unlikely to crash 10%+ in a normal week without major news.

### Should you?
**No — for a piggy bank during active day trading, probably not.**

### Reasons to Skip:
1. **Interest rate sensitivity** — Fed/news can move it 5%+ in a day
2. **Dividend timing risk** — Ex-dividend dates matter; holding through wrong date = missing payout
3. **Opportunity cost** — Capital locked while day trading other stocks
4. **No stop-loss friendly** — You'd need a wide stop (~$9.50) which creates big downside

---

## Alternative Recommendations

### If you want a "safer" piggy bank for 1 week:
1. **Stay in cash** — Wait for better entry on day trades
2. **Money market (SGOV/OUSXX)** — ~4-5% yield, zero price risk
3. **Short-term Treasuries (BIL)** — Near-term safety
4. **If you must play REITs:** Consider **NEW** or **NLY** — slightly different risk profiles

### If you want upside without rate risk:
- **GGB** — industrial, not rate-sensitive
- **Small crypto stocks** (MARA, RIOT) — more predictable volatility for day trades

> **Skip AGNC for the piggy bank this week.**
