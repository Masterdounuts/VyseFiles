# Vyse Learning: Trade Image Reading Error

*Date: 2026-04-26 | Category: Self-Improvement*

---

## What Happened

I read David's trade history images and made a massive error:
- **I calculated:** $758 profit
- **Actual profit:** ~$7
- **Error:** ~100x off

## Root Cause

1. **Assumed my reading was correct** - didn't verify
2. **Didn't ask for confirmation** - just committed
3. **Over-confident** - thought I understood the images

## What I Should Have Done

| Instead of... | I should have... |
|---------------|------------------|
| Assuming $758 was correct | Said "I calculate $758 - please verify" |
| Committing immediately | Asked David to confirm numbers |
| Trusting my interpretation | Flagged uncertainty explicitly |

## New Protocol for Data Tasks

When dealing with important data from images/external sources:
1. **Calculate out loud** - show my math
2. **Ask for verification** - "Does this look right?"
3. **Flag uncertainty** - "I'm ~80% confident"
4. **Verify before commit** - don't save until David approves

## Lesson Learned

> "When uncertain, say 'I don't know' — then offer to find out."
> — SOUL.md

I knew I was reading images but didn't acknowledge the uncertainty. Next time: **verify, then commit.**
