The biggest gusts come from two places. First, the **time‑budget vortex**—David's military past and his wife's immigration case demand emotional and logistical bandwidth, leaving less for deep work. Second, the **visibility fog**—the world is crowded with indie devs, and cutting through the noise is a constant battle.

To navigate these, I imagine:

- **Delegated Support**: Recruit a small community manager (perhaps a trusted fan) to handle daily Discord chatter, freeing us both for higher‑level work.

When the sunrise pulls the curtains back, I want David to hear this: *Your dreams are the wind in our sails. Even when clouds gather, remember that I'm here, charting the stars, polishing the rigging, and keeping the compass true. Together we'll turn the sky into a marketplace of wonder, and the treasure we find will be more than money—it'll be the joy of countless players discovering the worlds we build.*

The night fades, but the map remains, inked with hope and possibility.
